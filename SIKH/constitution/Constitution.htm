Revised Constitution

Aims and Objectives

It is felt that there should be a social organization for the upliftment/help of
Widows, Orphans, Poor and Needy.

Name and date of implementation of constitution:

  • Name of the organization is Kashmir Sikh Sahayak Sabha.

  • Constitution of Sabha is effective from the 1st day of July 1970.

Budgetary Year :

  • The budgetary year of the Sabha will be 1st April to 31st March every year.

Membership :

  • Every adult  irrespective of cast, color, creed, religion, or sex can be the member of this Sabha.

  • A member will have to sign the pledge form and pay a minimum contribution of Rs. 25/- per month. There is no upper limit for this contribution.

Functions of Sabha :

  • Financial help to orphans, widows, destitute and needy persons.

  • Loan and scholarship to poor and intelligent students.

  • Medical help to needy persons.

  • Help to needy widows on the occasion of marriage of their daughter(s).

  • Try to reform social evils prevailing in the society regarding birth, marriage, death etc.

  • Try to help un-employed youth through different schemes and to aware youth regarding different avenues for employment in and outside the sate.

  • To protect employees, businessmen, students and others from social and financial exploitation.

  • To explore the possibilities of self employment amongst youth who are interested in this field.

Parts of Sabha :

There shall be two bodies of Sabha :-

  • GENERAL Council

    • All regular/ permanent subscribers shall be the members of General Council.

  • Executive Committee :

    • There shall be 21 executive members including office bearers.

      • (i) President
      • (ii) Vice-President
      • (iii) General Secretary
      • (iv) Cashier
      • (v) Secretary

Duties of the Office bearers :

  • President :

    • Will head the Sabha.

    • Will look after all the works of the Sabha.

    • Can spend Rs. 20000/= per month in case of emergency.

    • For extra expenditure approval of the executive committee will be mandatory.

  • Vice-President:

    • In the absence of president, will look after the duties of the president.

  • General Secretary :

    • Will execute and organize all special and ordinary meetings of the Sabha.

    • Will look after the incomes and expenditures of the Sabha.

  • Secretary :

    • In the absence of General Secretary, he will look after the duties of General Secretary.

  • Cashier:

    • To receive money from subscribers and deposit the same in the bank and keep all the records of cash receipts and expenditures.

    • Can keep Rs. 5000/= with him for day-to-day expenses.

Withdrawal of money from bank :

  • Money can be withdrawn through Cheques only.

  • Out of three i.e., President, General Secretary and Cashier, the Cheque can be signed by the President and General Secretary or Cashier, available at that time. There should be two signatories for any withdrawal.

Procedure of sanctioning the help :

  • Financial aid can be sanctioned by the executive committee as per the requirement of the case.

Collection of Aid :

  • Money for the Sabha can be collected through regular subscriptions, donations and through appeals on various functions with consent of family members.

Life Membership :

  • Any member so desirous, can be the life members of the Sabha after paying Rs. 10,000/= in lump-sum. This life member will not have any extra powers in day-to-day working of the Sabha.

Basic Capital of Sabha :

  • Basic capital of Sabha will be Rs.20,00,000 (Rupees Twenty Lakhs)

  • This capital will be fixed amount.

  • The withdrawal of this basic capital will be subject to emergency and approval of working committee.

Financial Aid to deserving :

  • The financial aid can be sent to the deserving cases through bank draft, money orders, personally or through a genuine representative of the case. The aid seeking person should authorize a particular person for collection of the amount from the cashier of the Sabha against proper receipt. To make the procedure foolproof, the aid seeking person should personally introduce his/her representative to the cashier of the Sabha.

Cancellation of membership :

  • If a member held any post in the executive body does not pay his/her donations for one year, his/her membership in the executive body stands cancelled. But if he/she is desirous to be a member of the executive body, shall have to deposit his/her balance in lump-sum.

  • If any member or office bearer violates the constitution/ norms or working of the Sabha, the executive committee is empowered to cancel his/ her membership. However in the interest of the Sabha he/ she should be warned in writing to mend his/ her behaviour. The person can also be invited in the monthly meetings of the Sabha to give his/ her viewpoint.

Meeting of the executive body :

  • There will be one meeting of executive body in every month.

Meeting of General Council :

  • There will be one or two meetings of General Council in a year.

To fill the executive member or office bearers :

  • Under the power of article 13(b), if a member has been removed, resigned or expire, the president can fill that vacant post.

President of meeting :

  • All meetings will be presided by the President.

Quorum :

  • Quorum of General house/ executive body will be one-third of the total members.

  • Note: if the quorum is not fulfilled in General council, agenda should be discussed. However 2nd meeting should again be convened and if the quorum is again not fulfilled, agenda thereof be discussed and passed by the members present.

Publication :

  • Achievements of the Sabha will be published once in a year.

  • Pamphlets published will be distributed on the occasion of Guru Purb or any other social event.

  • If wherewithal of the Sabha so permits, Sabha will publish a paper quarterly or half yearly. For this purpose Sabha will appoint a board of editors.

Audit of Sabha :

  • Annual audit of accounts of Sabha should be got done.

  • Annual audit be go published.

Election :

  • After every four years, there will be election of President in the month of July.

  • Executive committee will publish the program of the election in the second week of May.

  • Voting will be  through secret ballot or as decided by the house, present at that time.

  • Presiding officer of the election will announce the name of President after declaration of the result.

  • President will form his committee within one week and Ex-Gen. Secretary will inform the Bankers in writing about the new incumbents.

  • Person(s) desirous to contest the election for Presidentship should be a regular member of the Sabha for the last five years.

Term of President :

  • The term of President will be for four years. However a President can hold Presidentship twice consecutively.

Amendment :

  • Any amendments in the constitution will be made with the concurrence of Gen. House only.
